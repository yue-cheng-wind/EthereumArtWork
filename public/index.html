Hello! My name is Alice.

I was born in Barnstaple in South West England in 1995. Having grown up both next to the sea and in close proximity to two National Parks my love of the outdoors grew tremendously. Throughout my life, I have always been fascinated by images and how the world is represented through the eyes of others. Naturally, I was drawn to painting. The interest was cultivated during my teenage years and further developed when I opted to take my Bachelors Degree in Art.

After completing my degree, I delved even more into my painting practice, which involved loss and regeneration of life. The images, that I take on my walks in the landscape, are only part of the narrative. I use the practice of burying my film negatives in the earth to allow the natural environment a voice. It is a collaboration between the artist and the land. I cherish this connection with nature. I feel that the artistic exchange between the land and the artist opens up many opportunities.

I have also trained as a teacher, specializing in Further Education. I teache 16-18-year-olds in order to help them foster the same enthusiasm that I have for art. I am also pursuing a Master’s Degree in Photography at Plymouth University alongside her teaching.

I was selected as one of the Graduates featured in Source Magazine (2014), Ffotogallery Cardiff. In 2015, I was a finalist South West Graduate Prize. My series called ‘Biosigna’ or Life Signals has been exhibited in London as well as in Bristol.
